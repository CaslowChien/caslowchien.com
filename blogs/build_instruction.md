# Everyone Should Learn Vibe Coding

> *“Vibe coding feels terrifying — not because it’s hard, but because it makes years of learning feel… pointless.”*

---

## The Fear No One Talks About

For anyone studying a computer-science–related degree, learning vibe coding can feel terrifying. Not because it’s difficult, but because it seems to trivialize all our years of learning how to code.

I remember when **DALL·E 1** first came out. I felt special. I was the only one around me who understood what AI was capable of.

Now? Even an alien — if they exist — probably knows what ChatGPT is.

---

## The Existential Crisis Phase

For a while, I was genuinely upset.

Why would anyone hire a junior data scientist if AI can do everything faster?

What were my *years* of learning to code for — if a 7-year-old can now “code” what I can?

That mindset followed me into my part-time internship at **Vizuro**, where they gave me access to an AI subscription.

I hesitated.  
Then I tried it.  
Then everything changed.

---

## The Turning Point

Once I stopped fighting it and started *working with it*, something clicked.

With vibe coding, I suddenly felt like I could build anything.

The fear turned into excitement.  
The resistance turned into curiosity.  
The dread turned into momentum.
